# Estimator recovery — a learned per-dimension judge recovers followability where IFS fails

**Date:** 2026-06-23. **Status: hardened, folded into the WMT 2026 paper (§7).**

The floor paper's negative result is that the surface IFS metric does not track human
instruction-followability (item-level Pearson r≈0.11) and _inverts_ the system ranking
(system-level Spearman(human, IFS) = −0.80 on the full set). This analysis tests the positive
counterpart on data already on disk — the cached LLM-judge labels — with **no new API calls**.

- Code: `src/eval/estimator_ranking.py` (reuses `correlate.williams_test` / `bootstrap_ci` and
  the `ifs` module; reads `llm_judge_cache.json` + `ratings_key.csv` + `ratings_filled.csv`).
- Result: `experiments/results/estimator_ranking.json`. Paper table: `paper/tables/estimator_recovery.tex`
  (auto-generated by `make_tables.py:estimator_table`, label `tab:estimator`).
- Reproduce:


## What the estimator is

Keep IFS's four dimensions (step order, action, entity, quantity), but replace the surface,
copy-counting estimator with a **learned** one: an LLM judge (Gemini 2.5 Flash, temp 0,
JSON-constrained) that reads source + translation and labels each dimension followable/not. The
composite is the mean of the four. Same 150 human-rated items, same followability target, same
correlation machinery as the IFS validation — the only change is _how the dimensions are estimated_.

## Finding 1 — recovers the segment-level signal

| Predictor of human followability  | Pearson r | 95% CI        | Williams p vs IFS   |
| --------------------------------- | --------- | ------------- | ------------------- |
| IFS (source-anchored)             | 0.11      | [−0.04, 0.27] | —                   |
| **Learned estimator (composite)** | **0.54**  | [0.37, 0.67]  | **<0.001** (t=4.66) |

The estimator predicts followability where IFS cannot, and the Williams test confirms the gap is
significant. Per-dimension, the biggest gains are the procedural dimensions IFS approximates
worst: action r=0.44, entity r=0.43.

## Finding 2 (headline) — recovers the system ranking

System-level Spearman of per-system means against the human ranking:

- **IFS: ρ = −0.40** (inverts on these 150 items; −0.80 on the full rating set) — crowns the
  fine-tuned NLLB humans rate least followable.
- **Learned estimator: ρ = +0.80** — restores Gemini and Google Translate to the top, pushes the
  fine-tuned NLLB down. Only the two lowest-rated systems are swapped.

## Finding 3 — the recovery is NOT self-preference

The judge shares a model family with one evaluated system (Gemini). Control: drop Gemini's
translations entirely, re-run on the remaining 3 systems (n=113):

- Learned estimator **r = 0.55** [0.37, 0.69], Williams **p < 0.001** — holds, even rises.
- Ranking **ρ = +0.50** vs IFS's −0.50 — still flips sign.

Honest wrinkle (reported in the paper Limitations): the judge is more lenient than humans on
every dimension and scores all 37 Gemini items a perfect 1.0, so its _absolute_ scores
over-credit fluent output. This caps per-dimension agreement (φ) and leaves headroom for
calibration or a cross-family judge. The _predictive_ claim holds on held-out systems regardless.
