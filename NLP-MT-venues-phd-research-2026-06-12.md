## SECTION 1: TACL — Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics

**Official page:** https://transacl.org/index.php/tacl/about
**MIT Press:** https://direct.mit.edu/tacl

### Submission Schedule

Rolling submissions, deadline on the **1st of every month** year-round. No embargo on arXiv preprints — you can post before or during submission. Uniquely flexible vs. conference cycles.

### Review Timeline

From the official About page and ACL Admin Wiki 2022Q1 report (covering 2021 data):

- Assignment to action editor: ~7 days
- Reviewer selection: ~7 days
- Review period: **3 weeks**
- Action editor decision: ~7 days
- **Time to first decision: ~50–60 days (6–8 weeks)**; confirmed mean 46.0 days, median 44 days in 2021

**Time to final acceptance** is less predictable:

- Papers receiving a (b)-decision (accept pending revision): typically 2–3 months more
- Papers receiving a (c)-decision (reject, invite resubmit within 3 months): another 3-week cycle after resubmission
- Overall first-submission-to-publication: **4–9 months** for most accepted papers
- (c)-path papers: up to 12 months

Source: https://www.aclweb.org/adminwiki/index.php/2022Q1_Reports:_TACL_Journal
Practitioner writeup (2024): https://ryokamoi.github.io/post/2024-08-24-tacl/

### Decision Types

- **(a) Accept as-is:** ~6% of first decisions (2021)
- **(b) Accept pending revisions:** ~24% — conditional accept, 2-month revision window
- **(c) Reject, invite resubmit within 3 months:** ~48% — of those who resubmit, ~55% eventually accepted
- **(d) Reject with 1-year moratorium:** ~21%

### Acceptance Rate

The "19–25%" figure is accurate but needs unpacking:

- First-decision accept (a+b combined) from 2021 data: ~30.6%
- Effective rate after revision failure: **~22–28%**
- Third-party aggregate (Resurchify): cites 22.3% for a specific year
- TACL publishes ~84–98 papers/year (Volumes 10–12)
- For context: EMNLP 2024 main accepted 20.82% (6,105 → 1,271 papers); ACL 2025 main ~20–22%

**Bottom line: TACL effective acceptance rate ~22–28%, comparable to ACL/EMNLP main conference.**

### Conference Presentation

TACL papers may optionally be presented at ACL-sponsored conferences (ACL, EMNLP, NAACL, EACL). Key facts:

- **Presentation is optional** — authors choose whether to present
- Each conference sets its own deadline for TACL paper registration (e.g., NAACL 2025 required acceptance by January 25, 2025: https://transacl.org/index.php/tacl/announcement/view/113)
- Slot type (oral vs. poster) is assigned by program committee — no guaranteed oral
- EMNLP 2024: **38 TACL papers** presented alongside main conference papers

### Page Length

No strict page maximum. Papers use ACL-style formatting, expected to be conference-length (~8 pages main + references). Unlimited appendices explicitly permitted since 2024.

### Prestige

**This is where the evidence is clearest and most consistent.**

- **CORE ranking: A\*** — same tier as ACL, EMNLP, NAACL main conferences
- **2022 impact factor: 10.9** — ranked **1st out of 194 linguistics journals**, 11th out of 145 AI journals (JCR)
- **SJR:** rose from 1.985 (2022) to 4.006 (2023)
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transactions_of_the_Association_for_Computational_Linguistics

Ehud Reiter (University of Aberdeen, action editor at both venues): "When I look at CVs I put more value on a good journal paper than a good conference paper." His separate post explicitly notes TACL uses experienced researchers as action editors / reviewers vs. the highly variable (often PhD student) reviewer pool at ACL conferences.

- https://ehudreiter.com/2018/12/11/publish-in-journals/
- https://ehudreiter.com/2023/06/06/acl-vs-tacl-reviewing/

**Community consensus:** TACL papers are listed alongside ACL/EMNLP main papers in all venue rosters. Treated as equivalent-to or higher-than main conference by the field.

**For PhD admissions:** No admissions committee publishes an explicit rubric, but the universal community treatment is TACL = ACL long paper. A TACL paper that is also presented at ACL/EMNLP loses no visibility disadvantage.

**Honest caveat:** Some faculty who are very conference-community-focused may give slight preference to an ACL/EMNLP main paper because it generated a poster/talk interaction during the conference. Presenting a TACL paper at \*ACL mitigates this entirely.

### Suitability for MT Eval/Method Paper

**Excellent.** TACL specifically rewards:

- Thorough, rigorous evaluation with complete experimental details
- Methodological contributions
- Papers that don't fit neatly into a conference's topical themes (longer write-up, more datasets, more ablations)
- Agentic / system papers that need space to explain the pipeline

## SECTION 2: Computational Linguistics Journal (MIT Press / ACL)

**Official page:** https://cljournal.org
**MIT Press:** https://direct.mit.edu/coli
**Submissions:** https://submissions.cljournal.org/index.php/cljournal
**SciRev (reviewer experience data):** https://scirev.org/journal/computational-linguistics/

### Submission Deadlines

Rolling — no fixed deadlines, accepts year-round.

### Review Timeline

From SciRev (reviewer-reported) and the former EIC's published retrospective (https://direct.mit.edu/coli/article/49/4/773/118910/):

- **Time to first decision: ~34–47 days** (confirmed by EIC for 2022–2023)
- CL Journal homepage states average 46 days to first decision
- SciRev: most recent report shows 6.5 weeks to first review
- Total handling time for accepted papers: ~3.0 months (SciRev)

**The "years to acceptance" reputation is outdated.** It dates from pre-2020 editorial practices. Current review speed is comparable to TACL.

**However:** "3 months total handling time" = accepted papers. Papers requiring multiple major revision rounds can take 6–18 months from submission to final acceptance. The iterative revision process is more traditional (more rounds, higher bar for revision completion) than TACL's structured (a)/(b)/(c)/(d) system.

### Acceptance Rate

Not publicly disclosed ("n/a" on SciRev). The journal publishes ~40–60 papers per year with undisclosed submission volume. Community-estimated range: **15–25%**. Likely more selective than TACL for empirical NLP work because the CL journal scope filters out many submissions before review.

### Page Length

No strict limit. CL journal papers typically run **20–40+ pages** — appropriate for surveys, theoretical frameworks, or exhaustive empirical studies.

### Is It Too Theoretical for MT Method+Eval?

**Partially yes, with nuance.** The journal covers "theoretical modeling, empirical investigation, system design, and scalable methods" per its stated scope. Empirical NLP papers do appear in 2024–2025. But:

- The citation footprint in the active MT research community (practitioners, WMT participants, post-editing researchers) is **lower than TACL**
- Papers are expected to have either: strong theoretical framing, survey scope, or methodological generalizability beyond a single language pair
- A Burmese MT paper with agentic post-editing + novel metrics is **on the edge of CL's typical scope** — it would need to argue for theoretical generalizability of the metrics or the agentic approach, not just demonstrate results for Myanmar

### CORE Ranking

**A\*** — same tier as TACL and ACL main conferences.

### Tier Assessment

| Dimension                          | Rating                                                         |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Academic prestige (journal impact) | Tier 1                                                         |
| Empirical MT / NLP readership      | Lower than TACL                                                |
| PhD admissions signal              | Tier 1 (but slow; may not publish before application deadline) |
| Fit for agentic MT eval paper      | Poor unless strong theoretical framing                         |

**Bottom line: CL journal is wrong venue for this paper.** TACL is strictly better for your use case. Do not submit here.

## SECTION 3: WMT Research Track

**WMT 2025 official:** https://www2.statmt.org/wmt25/
**WMT 2024 official:** http://www2.statmt.org/wmt24/
**ACL Anthology (WMT 2024 proceedings):** https://aclanthology.org/2024.wmt-1.0.pdf

### Paper Types: Research Track vs. Shared Task

**Research papers (fully peer-reviewed):**

- Original scientific work on MT
- 6–10 pages (excluding references/appendices)
- Fully anonymized, double-blind review
- Can be submitted via ARR and committed to WMT
- Subject to double-submission restrictions

**System papers (shared task participation):**

- Describe one or more shared task submissions
- Not anonymized (linked to shared task entry)
- 4–6 pages
- **Poster-only** — no oral presentations
- NOT subject to double-submission restrictions

### Co-location and Proceedings

WMT is co-located with **EMNLP**:

- WMT 2023: EMNLP 2023, Singapore (December)
- WMT 2024: EMNLP 2024, Miami, Florida (November 15–16, 2024) — **134 total papers**
- WMT 2025: EMNLP 2025, Suzhou, China (November 8–9, 2025)

**CRITICAL FACT:** WMT papers appear in **separate WMT proceedings** ("Proceedings of the Ninth Conference on Machine Translation"), NOT in main EMNLP proceedings. They are indexed in ACL Anthology under the workshop/conference category. They are NOT EMNLP papers.

### Acceptance Rates (Research Track)

- **WMT 2023 research track:** ~18 research papers from ~50 submissions (~36%)
- **WMT 2024 research track:** ~25 research papers from ~54 submissions (~46%)

These higher rates reflect a self-selected, specialized submission pool — not lower standards.

### Prestige: Honest Assessment

**Within the MT community:** WMT is highly respected. Shared task findings papers are among the most-cited MT papers year over year. Research track papers are taken seriously by MT researchers.

**Outside the MT community / PhD admissions committees:** WMT is classified as a **workshop** in the ACL Anthology. To a generalist NLP admissions committee at CMU, Stanford, MIT, or UW, a WMT research track paper registers as a **workshop paper**, not an ACL/EMNLP main or TACL paper.

**Would submitting an ambitious agentic-MT paper to WMT research track be "underselling" it?**

**Yes.** A paper with novel metrics + agentic post-editing + systematic evaluation on a low-resource language is main-venue material. Submitting it to WMT as the primary venue caps its perceived prestige in PhD applications. The correct strategy: submit to TACL or ACL/EMNLP main, then present at WMT if accepted, or publish a companion system paper at WMT as secondary contribution.

**Exception:** If the paper is narrow in scope (MT-specific evaluation only, no generalizable methodology) and needs a realistic acceptance path, WMT research track is a credible fallback.

## SECTION 4: MT-Specialist Conferences — MT Summit, AMTA, EAMT

### MT Summit 2025

**Official:** https://mtsummit2025.unige.ch/
**Organizer:** IAMT (International Association for Machine Translation)

- **Date/location:** Geneva, Switzerland, June 23–27, 2025
- **Acceptance rate:** **49%** (28 papers: 14 oral + 14 poster, from ~57 submissions)
- **Source:** ACL Anthology proceedings
- **Audience:** MT practitioners and researchers, strong industry presence (localization, post-editing workflows)
- **CORE ranking:** B (some sources list unranked)

**PhD admissions tier: 2–3.** Substantially below ACL/EMNLP (A\*) and NAACL/EACL (A). Shows domain engagement but does not carry significant weight at CMU, MIT, Stanford, or UW.

### AMTA 2025

**Official:** https://amtaweb.org/amta-2025-virtual-conference-program/
**Date:** September 25, 2025 (virtual)

**CRITICAL FACT:** AMTA 2025 is **non-archival**. The conference website states accepted papers "will otherwise not be published" and "will not follow a double-blind peer review process."

This means AMTA 2025 produces **no archival publication**. A paper "presented at AMTA 2025" carries essentially **no bibliographic weight** for PhD applications — it is a professional symposium talk, not a peer-reviewed publication.

Historical archival AMTA (full conference in even years, biennial) does produce ACL Anthology proceedings, but the 2025 virtual symposium format eliminates this. Check **AMTA 2026** for the next full archival conference.

**PhD admissions tier: 3** even in archival years. **Irrelevant in 2025 (non-archival).**

### EAMT 2025/2026

- EAMT 2025 was absorbed into MT Summit 2025 (Geneva)
- **EAMT 2026:** https://eamt2026.org/ — location TBA
- Produces ACL Anthology-indexed proceedings; acceptance rates typically 40–55%
- **PhD admissions tier: 2–3.** Appropriate for practitioner-facing MT work; would undersell a strong research contribution.

### SEA-NLP / Southeast Asian NLP Workshops

**No dedicated, recurring SEA-NLP workshop** with persistent ACL affiliation exists as of June 2026. Past options:

- **SEALP (SouthEast Asian Language Processing):** Sporadic, not consistently recurring
- **SIGTYP:** Covers typologically diverse/low-resource languages, not SEA-specific
- **WILDRE:** Indian language resources — adjacent but not SEA-focused

**Adjacent journal option:** **ACM TALLIP** (Transactions on Asian and Low-Resource Language Information Processing) — specifically covers Asian low-resource languages. Lower prestige than TACL but appropriate for Burmese-specific corpus/evaluation work.

### When Does a Workshop Make Sense?

**Workshop as PRIMARY venue is right when:**

- Work is a corpus/dataset contribution needing community feedback before a full paper
- Presenting preliminary results en route to a larger paper
- The specific audience matters more than broad prestige (e.g., WAT for Burmese–Japanese MT)
- You have a companion contribution (system description, dataset, position piece) separate from the main paper

**Workshop as PRIMARY venue is WRONG when:**

- You have a novel method, novel metric, or systematic evaluation with reproducible results
- The paper is complete and publication-ready
- You are applying to top PhD programs where venue prestige is a factor

**Best strategy for your situation:** Submit main paper to TACL or ACL/EMNLP main. Submit companion corpus release or system paper to LoResMT or WAT as secondary contribution.

### Is "Published at TACL" Equivalent to "Accepted at ACL Long" for PhD Applications?

**Yes, in almost all cases.** CORE ranking, community recognition, and impact factor all place them at the same tier. The practical difference:

- ACL/EMNLP main = higher immediate visibility (thousands attend the conference)
- TACL = higher review quality signal

A TACL paper presented at ACL or EMNLP loses no visibility disadvantage.

**Specific evidence:**

- CORE: both TACL and ACL are A\*
- TACL 2022 impact factor 10.9, ranked 1st/194 linguistics journals
- Ehud Reiter: "I put more value on a good journal paper than a good conference paper" (source: https://ehudreiter.com/2018/12/11/publish-in-journals/)
- ACL community consensus (Quora, Reddit threads): TACL listed alongside ACL/EMNLP main in all venue rosters

### Low-Resource Language Papers at Top Venues

The bar has risen and it is now viable. Evidence:

- **BURMESE-SAN** (Burmese-specific NLP): appeared in **Findings of NAACL 2025** (https://arxiv.org/html/2602.18788v1)
- **Myanmar XNLI**: arXiv paper targeting ACL/NAACL venues (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.09645)
- Low-resource MT papers routinely appear at ACL/EMNLP main in 2023–2025

Novelty of method/metric remains the primary filter — the low-resource angle alone is not sufficient, but it differentiates you within a field where agentic MT is already appearing (TACTIC arXiv 2025, Ladder arXiv 2024, post-editing with LLMs at NAACL 2024).

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

**Your paper profile:** MT evaluation/method paper, Burmese/Myanmar (low-resource), agentic post-editing, novel metrics. Target: top PhD programs (CMU, MIT, Stanford, UW, ETH Zurich, EPFL).

### Primary Submission: TACL

**Submit to TACL first.** Reasons:

1. **Rolling monthly deadlines** — submit as soon as paper is ready, no waiting for a conference cycle
2. **~50-day time to first decision** — fast enough for PhD applications on a Dec 2026 / Jan 2027 timeline
3. **Review quality** — experienced action editors are better equipped to evaluate a thorough eval paper with multiple metrics + an agentic system than rushed conference reviewers who may be unfamiliar with Myanmar MT
4. **Page flexibility** — unlimited appendices (since 2024); publish full evaluation methodology, error analysis, corpus statistics
5. **Prestige = ACL/EMNLP main** — no downside for PhD applications
6. **Optional presentation** — present at next available \*ACL conference after acceptance, gaining full conference visibility without surviving that conference's competitive acceptance cycle

### Fallback: ACL 2026 / EMNLP 2026 Main

If TACL returns a (d)-rejection (moratorium):

- Pivot to ARR → commit to **EMNLP 2026** (Budapest, October 2026)
- Or directly to **ACL 2026** (main deadline typically February–March 2026)

**Do not target Findings as a primary strategy.** Accept a Findings decision if offered (it's Tier 1.5 and better than workshop-only), but do not design the submission expecting it.

### Secondary / Companion: LoResMT 2026 at EACL

Submit a companion paper or corpus release to **LoResMT 2026** (EACL 2026, March 28, Rabat):

- Deadline: **December 26, 2025**
- Establishes community presence in low-resource MT
- Gets your dataset/benchmark in front of the researchers who will cite it
- Burmese is underrepresented in LoResMT — would attract attention

Label clearly as a workshop paper. This complements, does not replace, the main venue submission.
